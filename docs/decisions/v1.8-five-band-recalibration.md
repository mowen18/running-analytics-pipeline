# Revision v1.8 — 2026-07-22 — Five temperature bands: split the open top band at NOAA heat-index anchors (Addendum to D14)

Status: Adopted 2026-07-22. Docs-before-code: this addendum lands as a
docs-only commit; the recalibration (one cohesive commit), the
dry-bulb label sweep, and the verification postscript follow in
separate commits on the same branch.

## Rationale

v1.7 moved banding to apparent temperature and proved zero movers —
but it also left the open-ended `> 70°F` band holding 11 of the 14
weather-matched valid runs. The feels-like scale runs hotter than
dry-bulb in a Texas summer, so the top band stopped discriminating:
a 71°F evening jog and a 95°F afternoon run banded identically. v1.8
splits the top band twice; the seed bands become `< 50°F`, `50–70°F`,
`70–80°F`, `80–90°F`, `> 90°F`. The banding INPUT does not change
(apparent_temperature_f, per v1.7), the 50 and 70 boundaries do not
move, and the pseudo-bands are untouched.

Supersession acknowledgment: the v1.7 addendum recorded "band bounds,
band count, or new thresholds of any kind" as out of scope and listed
the band_key vocabulary, its accepted_values test, and the app's
ordinal color ramp among its non-changes. Past addenda are immutable,
so this revision states the supersession here: v1.8 deliberately
changes all four, under the stopping rule below.

## What changes

The seed, old → new (bounds inclusive at 1 dp, per the existing edge
convention — 70.0 stays in 50–70 exactly as before; 80.0 lands in
70–80; 90.0 lands in 80–90):

| band_key | band_label | min | max | sort_order |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| cold | < 50°F | — | 49.9 | 1 (unchanged) |
| mild | 50–70°F | 50.0 | 70.0 | 2 (unchanged) |
| warm | 70–80°F | 70.1 | 80.0 | 3 (bounds narrowed, label updated) |
| hot | 80–90°F | 80.1 | 90.0 | 4 (new) |
| very_hot | > 90°F | 90.1 | — | 5 (new) |

Key/label contract: band keys are stable machine identifiers and
labels are display strings — so `warm` keeps its key under the new
"70–80°F" label (renaming it would churn every consumer for no
semantic gain), and the new bands take keys `hot` and `very_hot`.
The accepted_values contract on `mart_efficiency_by_temp_band.band_key`
grows by exactly those two keys.

Both new boundaries carry a dual justification:

- **External anchor.** NOAA's heat-index scale enters "caution" at
  80°F and "extreme caution" at 90°F. The boundaries are adopted, not
  invented.
- **Empirical gap.** The observed feels-like distribution (14 matched
  valid runs, 2026-07-22) has gaps at 74.3–80.4 and 88.7–94.6, so
  both cuts are insensitive — no current run's assignment depends on
  the exact value chosen inside either gap.

Dashboard changes (see the D19 section): the ordinal ramp extends to
five blues on the same documented ramp, the by-band chart's height
grows for seven bars, and the dry-bulb label sweep lands ("Avg air
°F" headers; two dry-bulb tooltips relabeled).

## Stopping rule

A band boundary requires BOTH an external anchor AND an empirical gap
in the observed distribution. Neither alone suffices: an anchor
without a gap would cut through data and make assignments sensitive
to a threshold this project did not measure; a gap without an anchor
is numerology. No further candidates exist — NOAA's next threshold
(103°F, "danger") lies beyond any completed run, and finer cuts would
create bands that cannot reach usable n at this data volume. This
rule is what distinguishes principled recalibration from threshold
creep, and any future band change must satisfy it.

## Survivorship caveat

Runs above ~90°F feels-like are systematically cut short and fail the
15-minute validity floor: the excluded-run cluster sits at 94.1–97.0
(five runs, every one excluded for "moving time under 15 minutes"),
while exactly one >90 run was ever completed. The `> 90°F` band
therefore only ever contains extreme-heat runs that were finished —
its statistics are survival-censored, and no efficiency comparison
against it may be read at face value. The band exists to make that
censoring visible: n=0 or n=1 IS the finding, in the same spirit as
the empty-band-is-data rule. (Concretely, of the 6 rows that relabel
"> 90°F" in mart_run_quality, 5 are excluded runs.)

## What does NOT change

- **The banding input** — apparent_temperature_f, per v1.7; dry-bulb
  fallback remains forbidden.
- **The 50 and 70 boundaries**, the 1-dp inclusive edge convention,
  and the seed column names (min_temperature_f / max_temperature_f).
- **The macro** (temperature_band_range) and the run-grain ladder
  totality test's structure — neither enumerates band values.
- **The pseudo-bands**: keys, labels, definitions, and their
  hardcoded sort orders 98/99 (new seed rows take 4 and 5).
- **Every avg_temperature_f column** stays dry-bulb;
  avg_apparent_temperature_f stays mart-only (v1.7).
- **The D19 allow-list and three-view cap** — no mart is added or
  removed.
- **Explicitly deferred**: the NaN-in-tooltip rendering edge flagged
  in v1.7 is a separate micro-commit, not this branch; dew point
  remains a possible future revision under the stopping rule.

## Consistency contract

`assert_temperature_bands_cover_all_temps` (every 0.1°F from −30 to
130 must land in exactly one band) and the accepted_values test guard
the new seed — a gap, an overlap, or an unknown key fails the build.
The run-grain totality test's fixtures gain three boundary witnesses:
feels-like exactly 80.0 (lands 70–80 — the inclusive top edge,
mirroring the real May-29 70.0 case), exactly 90.0 (lands 80–90), and
95.0 (exercises the new unbounded top). These EXTEND an
already-green partition test's coverage; **no red proof is run and
none is owed** — no defect exists, the ladder's structure is
unchanged, and manufacturing a red against correct code would prove
nothing. This is a recorded, deliberate deviation from the red-first
convention, which applies to guards catching real defects.
